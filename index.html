Ingredient Brand A/B Testing for Cost Optimization (Multi-Outlet F&B)

Experimental

Conducted a 4-outlet A/B test to compare two ingredient brands and identify the most cost-effective option while maintaining product taste.

Methodology

  • Assigned Brand A to Outlets A & B and Brand B to Outlets C & D for 2 weeks, then swapped brands to reduce outlet bias.
  • Worked with operations to adjust recipe ratios based on each brand's characteristics.
  • Tracked product sales, ingredient usage, ingredient cost, and customer complaints.
  • Used complaint-based monitoring, treating zero complaints as successful taste consistency.
  • Compared results across both phases to evaluate ingredient efficiency and cost per product.

Results

  • Selected higher-yield ingredient brand with no increase in customer complaints.
  • Achieved approximately 15% reduction in ingredient cost per product.

Ingredient Reorder Point Optimization (Inventory Planning – F&B)

Operational

Developed a data-driven minimum stock model to trigger ingredient purchasing early, reducing emergency orders while improving cost efficiency, storage usage, and cashflow.

Methodology

  • Analyzed historical ingredient usage using a 10-week weighted moving average, placing higher emphasis on the latest 3 weeks to reflect current demand trends.
  • Estimated average daily consumption and supplier lead time to calculate minimum stock (reorder point) thresholds.
  • Implemented alert levels to prompt purchasing before inventory reached critical levels.
  • Monitored stock movement and adjusted thresholds based on actual usage.

Results

  • Reduced emergency panic purchases.
  • Achieved estimated 5–10% raw ingredient cost savings through proactive planning.
  • Improved warehouse space utilization by avoiding overstock and stockouts.
  • Improved cashflow by reducing excess inventory and enabling more predictable purchasing cycles.
